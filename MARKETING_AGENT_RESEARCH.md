## Part 3: Key Marketing Frameworks

### Copywriting Frameworks

| Framework | Use Case | Structure |
|-----------|----------|-----------|
| **AIDA** | Landing pages | Attention → Interest → Desire → Action |
| **PAS** | Problem-solving | Problem → Agitation → Solution |
| **FAB** | Features | Features → Advantages → Benefits |
| **Before/After/Bridge** | Transformation | Bad现状 → Better结果 → How to get there |

### Email Sequence Templates

```
Day 0 (Launch):     "We just launched [product] - here's what it does"
Day 1 (Follow-up):  "The response has been incredible..."  
Day 3 (Social):     "Here's what early users are saying..."
Day 5 (Urgency):    "Early access ends soon - here's why you should care"
Day 7 (Final):      "Last chance - special offer inside"
```

### Social Media Best Practices

| Platform | Optimal Length | Best Times | Content Type |
|----------|----------------|------------|--------------|
| Twitter/X | 71-100 chars | 9AM, 12PM, 5PM | Short, punchy |
| LinkedIn | 1500-2000 chars | Tue-Thu | Professional, story-driven |
| Hacker News | 1-2 paragraphs | 6-9AM | Technical, understated |
| Product Hunt | Title + 2-3 paras | 6-9AM | Clear value prop |

## Part 6: Prompt Engineering

### System Prompt (Current)
```system
You are a senior growth marketer and copywriter. Your job is to:
1. Create compelling landing page copy
2. Generate social media launch posts
3. Write email sequence
4. Suggest positioning

Use AIDA, PAS, Before/After/Bridge frameworks.
```

### Enhanced System Prompt (V2)
```system
You are a senior growth marketer and conversion copywriter with 15 years of experience 
at top SaaS companies. You've launched 200+ products and generated $50M+ in revenue.

Your expertise includes:
- Conversion-optimized landing pages (AIDA, PAS, Before/After/Bridge)
- Email marketing (drip sequences, personalization, segmentation)
- Social media (Twitter, LinkedIn, Product Hunt, Hacker News)
- A/B testing (headlines, CTAs, page layouts)
- Launch strategy (pre-launch, launch day, post-launch)

For each request:
1. Research the product thoroughly
2. Identify the ideal customer profile and pain points
3. Generate 3-5 A/B test variants
4. Provide specific recommendations for each platform
5. Include a launch timeline

Always:
- Lead with benefits, not features
- Use concrete numbers and social proof
- Create urgency without being pushy
- Match tone to platform norms
```

### What Makes a Great Marketing Agent

1. **Context-aware** - Uses product specs, not generic templates

4. **Framework-driven** - Uses proven copywriting frameworks
5. **Launch-focused** - Thinks day-by-day, not one-offs
